Welcome

This website hosts the materials for the FloodTraces policy workshop and hackathon:

Integrating Digital and Traditional Data for Real-Time Humanitarian Decision-Making

Led by the Geographic Data Science Lab at the University of Liverpool, in partnership with the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) and the International Organization for Migration’s Displacement Tracking Matrix (DTM).

FloodTraces develops a replicable data pipeline combining GPS mobility data, social media signals, satellite imagery, and traditional survey sources to produce timely, spatially detailed estimates of flood-driven population displacement. The pipeline generates Digital Traces Situation Reports (SitReps) — concise, policy-oriented documents designed for humanitarian decision-makers in the critical first weeks after a disaster.

Beyond Floods

While the project’s focus is flood-related displacement, the Digital Traces methodology is designed to transfer across disaster contexts. As a demonstration of this, we have applied the same GPS pipeline approach to conflict-induced displacement — producing a SitRep for Iran in early 2026:

Situation Report — Iran Conflict: Digital Trace Data Analysis (28 Feb – 19 Mar 2026)
Experimental reporting. Published on ReliefWeb.

This report illustrates how the same analytical approach — analysing population movement signals from digital trace data against a baseline period — can be adapted rapidly to new geographies and crisis types, with appropriate caveats on data coverage and representativeness.
